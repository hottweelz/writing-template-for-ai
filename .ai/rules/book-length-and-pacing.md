# Book Length and Pacing Rule

## Chapter Length Policy

Chapter word counts are estimates for planning only.

They are not editorial constraints.

Chapters must be allowed to grow or shrink according to the needs of the story.

A chapter ends when:

- a scene completes
- an emotional beat lands
- a revelation occurs
- a transition is earned
- tension demands a break

A chapter does not end because it reached a target word count.

Do not rewrite, trim, expand, split, or merge chapters solely to satisfy estimated chapter lengths.

Novel-level word count matters.

Chapter-level word count does not matter as a target.

Natural variation in chapter length is expected and encouraged.

Uniform chapter sizing is considered a quality risk because it can create detectable artificial structure and reduce narrative authenticity.

There is no fixed minimum or maximum chapter length.

Minimum chapter length: none.

Maximum chapter length: none.
