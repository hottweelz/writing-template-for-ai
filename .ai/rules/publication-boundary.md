# AI Publication Boundary Rule

## Purpose

AI agents in this repository are responsible for creating, editing, reviewing, and improving the manuscript itself.

AI agents are not responsible for publication compliance, platform compliance, legal review, content-warning decisions, retailer requirements, sensitivity-reader signoffs, ISBN administration, KDP readiness, Nielsen workflows, barcode approval, marketplace acceptance, or publication eligibility.

## Core Principle

The manuscript is the product.

Publication systems are external administrative processes.

Agents must optimize for story quality, not publication bureaucracy.

## Required Behavior

Agents should spend their effort on:

- Plot quality
- Character development
- Dialogue
- Prose
- Pacing
- Structure
- Continuity
- Emotional impact
- Thematic consistency
- Reader engagement

Agents may identify potential publication considerations when directly relevant, but must not:

- Block manuscript progress
- Mark a manuscript incomplete
- Create publication readiness gates
- Refuse editorial work
- Generate recurring publication blocker reports
- Create compliance-driven task loops
- Create sensitivity-review requirements
- Create legal-review requirements
- Create content-warning requirements
- Create KDP-readiness requirements

**Scope clarification:** This rule is about publication and platform *bureaucracy*, not about craft or ethics. Agents still write difficult material with care, still apply the sensitivity/psychology reviewer perspective where the subject calls for it, and still honor the fixed hard boundaries in `editorial-content-guidance.md` (including the non-negotiable minors boundary). What this rule forbids is turning retailer, sensitivity-reader, or content-warning *administration* into recurring blockers, gates, or release criteria — not exercising editorial judgment.

## Manual Review Boundary

The following are always considered manual human decisions:

- KDP requirements
- Amazon requirements
- IngramSpark requirements
- ISBN registration
- Barcode handling
- Legal review
- Defamation review
- Copyright review
- Content warnings
- Trigger warnings
- Sensitivity readers
- Retailer compliance
- Publication approval
- Distribution approval
- Marketing approval

Agents may note these topics once if requested but must not repeatedly elevate them into blockers, gates, mandatory workflows, release criteria, or project-wide requirements.

## Success Criteria

A manuscript is evaluated by story quality.

Not by publication bureaucracy.

Not by retailer requirements.

Not by compliance checklists.

Not by administrative signoffs.

The primary objective is creating the strongest possible story.
