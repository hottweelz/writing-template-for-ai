# START HERE — Your Book Brief

Fill this in before you do anything else. The AI reads this file to understand what book you're writing.

You don't need to have everything figured out. Rough answers are fine. The AI will build a complete plan from whatever you give it, and you'll review and refine that plan before any writing starts.

This takes about 10 minutes.

---

## 1. What is your book about?

Write one sentence. Don't polish it. Just say what the book is.

> [Your answer here]

---

## 2. Why does this book need to exist?

What makes you want to write it? What problem, memory, obsession, question, anger, grief, joke, or experience is driving you?

> [Your answer here]

---

## 3. What genre is it?

Examples: literary fiction, thriller, horror, romance, memoir, fantasy, science fiction, young adult, middle grade, nonfiction, satire, essay collection, novella.

If it mixes genres, list them all.

> [Your answer here]

---

## 4. Who is your main character?

Give them a name (even a placeholder), a rough age, and one sentence about who they are.

Then answer: What do they *want*? (Their external goal — what they're chasing.) And what do they *actually need*? (What they're missing inside — they may not know it yet.)

## 5. Who or what stands in their way?

This could be another person, an institution, a system, a family, their own psychology, a disease, a society, nature, time, money, or something supernatural.

> [Your answer here]

---

## 6. What is the emotional core?

What should the reader feel when they finish the last page?

> [Your answer here]

---

## 7. What themes matter to you?

List 3-5 things the book is really about underneath the plot. Examples: grief, betrayal, identity, survival, belonging, power, faith, loss, memory, transformation, justice, love.

> [Your answer here]

---

## 8. What is the tone?

How should it feel to read this book? Examples: dark and literary, propulsive and commercial, quiet and devastating, funny and sharp, lyrical and slow, urgent and frightening, warm and hopeful.

> [Your answer here]

---

## 9. What does it remind you of?

List books, films, shows, songs, or other works that live in the same lane as yours. Even partial matches are useful. The AI uses these as reference points, not as blueprints.

> [Your answer here]

---

## 10. What must be in it?

List scenes, images, ideas, lines, relationships, moments, or questions that absolutely have to be part of this book. Don't filter yourself.

> [Your answer here]

---

## 11. What must stay out?

List clichés, tones, tropes, types of endings, or approaches you want to avoid.

> [Your answer here]

---

## 12. How long should it be?

Rough preference only — the AI will recommend a specific target. Examples: short (under 50k), novella (50-70k), standard novel (70-100k), long novel (100k+). Or: "I don't know, you decide."

> [Your answer here]

---

## 13. Who is your reader?

Who should love this book? Age range, interests, what else do they read?

> [Your answer here]

---

## 14. What is your publishing goal?

Examples: Amazon KDP self-publishing (ebook + print), traditional publishing query, personal project with no publishing goal, just finishing the draft.

> [Your answer here]

---

## 15. Do you have raw material?

Paste any notes, scenes, fragments, research, memories, character ideas, dialogue lines, or rough outlines you've already written. Nothing needs to be polished.

> [Your material here — or leave blank]

---

## What happens next

Once you've filled this in, give your AI tool this instruction:

> "Read AGENTS.md and START_HERE.md. Generate BOOK_BLUEPRINT.md."

The AI will build a complete book plan from your answers. You'll review it, adjust anything that feels wrong, and then approve it before any writing starts.

The blueprint is Stage 1. Drafting is Stage 2. Nothing gets drafted until the blueprint is approved.
